# M1.7 — Selective control: uncertainty as an abstain gate for planning (spec)

## Question
M1.2 showed uncertainty as a planning **cost penalty** is null (penalizing uncertain plans ≠ better plans).
M1.6 showed uncertainty *is* a good **monitor** (selective prediction). M1.7 applies that to control: used as
an **abstain gate** rather than a cost, does uncertainty improve control **reliability under distribution
shift** — i.e., decline the tasks you'd fail, act on the ones you'd succeed?

## Method (no retrain; mirrors M1.6 at the episode level)
- Run **vanilla** CEM-MPC (the working M1.2 planner, action-scale 2.0) on `EPISODES` episodes; **half have
  noise-corrupted observations** every step (the current obs is corrupted, the goal is clean) — a controlled
  distribution shift the planner fails on.
- Per episode, record the **outcome** `best_reward` and two **confidence signals**:
  - **shell deviation** `mean_t |‖encode(obs_t)‖ − shell|` (OOD/epistemic).
  - **MC-variance** `mean_t Var_K[rollout(plan_t)]` (predictive uncertainty of the planned rollout).
- **Risk–coverage over episodes:** rank episodes by a signal (most-confident first), keep the top-`c`
  fraction (= "act on"), measure **risk `= −best_reward`** on the kept set. Summarize by **AURC** (lower =
  better). References: **oracle** (rank by true reward) and **random** (flat at mean risk).

## Signals compared
`shell`, `MC-variance`, `combined` (z-score sum), vs `oracle` (floor) and `random` (no-skill).

## Eval / verdict
- **WIN** — a gate's AURC ≪ random, and **combined** covers both axes (shell catches the corrupted
  episodes; MC-var the in-distribution hard ones) → *uncertainty-gating improves control reliability under
  shift*, even though it never improves nominal planning (the M1.2 null stands; this is the safety use).
- **NULL** — no gate beats random (would mean the signals don't separate failing episodes from succeeding
  ones, contradicting M1.6's OOD result; not expected, since shell cleanly flags the corruption).

Sanity printed: clean vs corrupted mean reward (corruption must actually hurt) and clean vs corrupted shell
deviation (the shift must be detectable).

## Code
- New `src/plan_gating.py`, reusing the M1.2 CEM machinery (`encode_one`, `rollout_final`, `cem`) + the
  monitor's corruption + the risk-coverage/AURC logic. Colab GPU. Heaviest M1.x script (CEM × episodes), but
  same shape as M1.2.

## Out of scope (YAGNI)
Per-step gating (no safe Push-T default action); uncertainty in the cost (M1.2 null); retraining.
