# M1.5 — Drift-aware surprise head (spec)

## Question
M1.4: a head trained on TRUE latents predicts one-step surprise (held-out Spearman +0.38), but at DEPLOY
— on the drifted maintained latent — no arm beats fixed-interval (PARTIAL). The binding constraint was the
**train/deploy distribution gap**. M1.5 removes that gap: train the head on *exactly the deploy
distribution*, and ask whether the PARTIAL becomes a WIN or a structural NULL.

## Method
- **Training pairs from the deploy distribution.** Free-run the maintained latent with **random looks**
  (per-step look prob `P_LOOK`, several passes per rollout). At each step record
  `(ẑ_{t-1}, a_{t-1}, h) → e_t`, where `h = steps since last look` (the drift of `ẑ_{t-1}`) and
  `e_t = ‖predict(ẑ-history, a) − true z_t‖` is the *realized* one-step error from the drifted estimate.
- **Head.** `DriftHead(z, a, h_norm) → log1p(e)`; same tiny MLP as M1.4 plus the scalar `h/T1`. No LeWM
  retrain.
- **`h` is given explicitly on purpose.** The head can trivially learn `e ≈ f(h)` (error grows with time
  since look) — which *is* fixed-interval. It beats fixed only if it also learns **state-dependent** drift
  rate from `(z, a)`.

## Eval (pre-registered)
1. **Predictability on the deploy distribution:** held-out Spearman(predicted `e`, true `e`). **Control:**
   Spearman(`h`, true `e`) — the trend a uniform schedule already exploits. The head is only interesting
   if it clears `h`-alone.
2. **Deploy:** drift-aware `learned` arm vs fixed-interval and oracle, matched budget, on held-out
   rollouts.

**Verdict:**
- **WIN** — `learned` beats fixed beyond SEM and approaches oracle → state-dependent drift IS readable
  from the maintained estimate. The thread's **first constructive positive**: calibrated WM uncertainty is
  actionable for sensing once the surprise predictor sees the deploy distribution.
- **NULL** — `learned ≈ fixed` even trained on the deploy distribution → the obstacle is **structural**
  (`h` ≡ uniform spacing; state-dependent divergence isn't readable off your own drift). Makes the
  M1.2–1.4 negative airtight.

## Out of scope (YAGNI)

(M1.5 keeps the single-MLP form to isolate the distribution-gap variable).
